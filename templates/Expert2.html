Interviewer: Can you share specific examples of AI tools that have been beneficial in your practice?

Dr. Patel: Certainly. IBM Watson for Oncology, for example, assists by analyzing extensive medical literature and patient data to provide evidence-based treatment recommendations. Another tool, PathAI, helps pathologists in diagnosing cancer from tissue samples. These AI tools have significantly improved diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning, leading to better patient outcomes.

Interviewer: What are the future prospects for AI in oncology?

Dr. Patel: The future of AI in oncology is very promising. We can anticipate further advancements in AI-driven diagnostics and treatment planning. AI could also play a role in drug discovery, identifying new therapeutic targets and accelerating the development of new cancer treatments. As technology evolves, AI will become even more integral to oncology, enhancing the quality of care and patient outcomes.
